A Man on a VanVan

About

Suzuki is one of the largest motorcylce and other power-sport vehicle manufactures in the world. They are well known for the quality and general affordability of their bikes. The Japanese-based company makes bikes ranging from small dirt bikes to the Hayabusa, one of the most well known "super sport" bikes in the world. However, some of the most fun to be had on two wheels can be atop a VanVan 200, Suzuki's jack of all trades, afordable and simple dual sport.

The term "dual-sport" applies to bikes that can go both on and off-road with ease. Some times, as is the case in Suzuki's highly demanded DR- line, a dual sport is more or less just a dirt bike design with some blinkers, tail lights, and a license plate holder bolted to the back. The VanVan, on the other hand, takes a retro yet simplistic take on the highly funtional class of bike.

Styling

The VanVan has a very simplistic and retro vibe coming off it, leaving it iviting to the learning and expierenced rider alike. With a medium deat height (30.3 in) a rider is can easily put his feet flat on the ground. While it does look sort of small, even playfull, the extra wide matched-tires give it just a little bit of mean-ness.

Suzuki left it all super simple when it came to designing this little beast. If you look at the speedometer display, it lacks any sort of fancy tech you might come to expect with bikes, especially dual-sports, on the instrument cluster; There is one analog dial that reads speed in MPH with an analog odometer, one light idicator for turn signals, one for High-Beams, and one fule indicator. Take a step back from the bike and you might notice that the actual frame of the bike is super simple too, to avoid weight. The engine is actually a stressed member in the frame of the bike: a pretty rare occurance, but once again, brilliantly simple by Suzuki. Up front theres one simple and retro head beam atop the extra wide front tire and fender. The back is similarly simple and retro tail light hanging above the "extra wide, 14-inch rear ballon-style tire". The seat is incredibly wide and spacious, either to carry a passenger or to leave plenty of room for the rider to move freely.

While in the 2018 and previous models there were a couple piant decals that played into the retro and fun nature of the bike, the newest 2019 model comes in either flat grey or metallic orange, with few decals or distractions from the bikes simple beauty.
